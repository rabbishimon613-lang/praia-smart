# Positioning

## Who it's for (in order)

1. **Brazilians in coastal cities planning a beach day** — primary use case, ~80% of summer traffic
2. **Surfers checking spots** — high engagement, vocal community, free marketing if they like us
3. **Tourists (BR domestic + international)** — Dec-Feb spike, low local knowledge, high conversion to featured venues
4. **Lifestyle decision-makers** — corporate event planners, brewery brand managers, event production. Latent audience but the most monetizable per session.

## When they use it

The **morning of**, on a phone, on the bus / Uber / before leaving the apartment.

Secondary: **the Friday before**, planning the weekend (this is what the `/{city}/fim-de-semana.html` pages target).

We are not used "in advance" the way Booking or Skyscanner are. The session is 30 seconds to 2 minutes. Decision-grade.

## The wedge

**Real-time + per-posto + integrated.**

Three things together. Nobody else has all three:

- **Climatempo:** real-time + city-level + integrated. Per-posto granularity = missing.
- **Surfline:** real-time + per-spot + surf-only. Integrated (água, agito, kid-friendliness) = missing.
- **INEA:** weekly + per-point + água-only. Real-time + integrated = missing.
- **YouTube live cams:** real-time + per-location + cam-only. Per-posto matching + integrated data = missing.
- **Google search:** integrated (in theory) + user-assembled + fragmented. Per-posto + clean = missing.

Our wedge is the **intersection** that nobody else built. Each existing player owns one axis; we own the cross-section.

## How we describe the product (in PT-BR, for marketing)

> *"praia smart é o painel da praia em tempo real. Tudo que você precisa saber pra escolher pra qual praia ir hoje: câmera ao vivo, ondas, vento, água, UV, movimento, balneabilidade oficial. Atualiza a cada 15 minutos. De graça, sem login."*

That's the elevator pitch in 40 words. Memorize it.

## How we describe the product (in English, for investors)

> *"praia smart is the real-time decision layer for the beach economy. We aggregate live cams, marine forecasts, water quality, and crowd estimates into a per-posto dashboard updated every 15 minutes. Brazilian users decide where to spend their beach day on us. Coastal venues pay us to be the answer."*

## The "don't be" list

Anti-patterns to avoid as we grow:

- **Don't be a dashboard.** Looks like a Bloomberg terminal = lose. The redesign already moved us away from this; protect it.
- **Don't be Yelp.** Reviews bring lawyers, moderation cost, and a different problem domain.
- **Don't be Instagram.** No DMs, no follow graph. Aggregation is OK; interaction isn't.
- **Don't be free forever.** Monetization is part of the product, not an afterthought. Featured slots are visible, labeled, and accepted because they help the user decide.
- **Don't be Brazil-only forever.** PT-speaking Atlantic is the obvious year-2 move. Don't get cozy in Rio.

## Brand vibe

- **Voice:** carioca / praiano. Friendly, never corporate. "tá bom pra surfar agora" not "condições ideais identificadas para a prática de surfe."
- **Visual:** light, breezy, "Brazilian beach at 10am." Coral + sea-blue + sand cream. Hand-drawn line-art glyphs. Burle Marx / Niemeyer / Bossa Nova album covers as mood reference.
- **Speed:** every page renders fast. Animations subtle. Mobile-first. No skeleton screens, no loading spinners — pre-rendered HTML or it doesn't ship.
- **Trust signal:** sources cited. "INEA · 4 dias atrás" appears next to water quality. We never hide that real-time data has lag — we honor it.

## What we'd never put in a tagline

- "Make every beach day perfect" → no
- "The world's largest beach database" → no
- "AI-powered beach intelligence" → absolutely no
- "Beach social network" → never
- "Find your perfect beach" → no, we tell them where to go

What we WOULD put:

- *"Pra onde a praia tá te chamando hoje?"*
- *"A praia agora, num lugar só."*
- *"De graça. Em tempo real. Pra todo Brasil."*

Steal these for headlines.
